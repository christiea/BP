Red Rocks

User Photos

Red Rocks reserve is a beautiful rocky walk on the coast of Wellington. As the name suggestd there are stunning red rocks caused by an increased iron concentration. Drive or walk the coast and see the locals. Sea lions sunbathe on the famed red rocks. Maintain your distance and respect the local.

Now to gain access to this group. Scan the QR code on the noticeboard by the public bathrooms and get snapping. Click "Add to the Bigger Picture" on the app and follow the directions on your screen to contribute to the 360 degree photo at the end of the trail.

Mt. Victoria

User Photos

The Mount Victoria lookout offers a breathtaking 360-degree scenic view of Wellington and a some scenic walking tracks.

The location offers many possible routes, either driving/public transport to the top or a short route, mostly under forest and covered or a longer but scenic route.

Wrights Reserve

User Photos

Located upon the hilltop in Karori. The fortress is an underground network of "tunnels, operations rooms and three gun emplacements."

Built during the World War Two; the location has become a significant historical location and many members of the public can be lead on a guided tour on open days. Otherwise you can guide yourself. From on top the hill is a beautiful lookout over the town.

Find the QR code to the right of the parking lot on the Wrights Reserve Map Board. Scan either using our in-app camera or your phone's camera to gain access to this group.
